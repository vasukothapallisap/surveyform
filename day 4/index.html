About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work in ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Beyond his scientific achievements, he was a beloved teacher, writer, and visionary who inspired millions of youth with his wisdom and humility.

His books, especially "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds", continue to motivate young minds across the nation. Dr. Kalam believed in the power of dreams and education, and his legacy lives on through the countless lives he touched.

“Dream is not that which you see while sleeping; it is something that does not let you sleep.” – Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
